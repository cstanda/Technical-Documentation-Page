What is online trading?

Online trading invloes the buying and selling of financial securities such as currencies, stocks, cryptocurrencies, etc through internet based systems with the intention to make profits.

Before the introduction of electronic interfaces, trading was mainly conducted through face-to-face interactions as traders would gather at different trading floors such as the New York or Nairobi Stock Exchange in order to execute trades in real time. Traders were also able to place orders through mail or fax which meant that trade executions would take longer. Newspapers and Financial Bulletins were used as sources for market news which lead to slow access to important market data in order to make decisions.

Internet based systems allow traders to access different markets any time from anywhere in the world and also allow real time access of data in order to make timely decisions. Many platforms have been created and proide traders with many features such as analytical tools, educational resources, trading algorithms and bots

Types of markets

The financial markets provide an avenue for buying and selling of securities such as stocks, foreign currencies, metals, cryptocurrencies, mutual funds, etc. The financial markets are divided into different categories each serving a specific purpose.

Market Characteristics
Stock/Equities Publicly traded companies list their shares to raise capital.
Bond Trading of debt securities e.g. Treasury bonds, municipal bonds, corporate bonds.
Forex Participants buy or sell currencies as they either speculate or hedge on exchange rates.
Derivatives Involves trading futures or options instruments that derive their value from other underlying assets. Derivatives are used for hedging, speculation or arbitrage.
Futures A trading agreements to buy or sell an asset (physical goods) at a given price on a future date. Futures trading derive their value from an underlying market.
Options Options contracts create a right but not an obligation to buy or sell an asset at a future date.
Commodities Trading is done on derivatives markets whereby instruments derive their value from underlying commodities market for selling primary goods. E.g. agricultural products (wheat, etc), energy products(crude oil, etc), precious metals (gold, etc).
Cryptocurrencies A decentralized market for digital assets such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc
Money Invole short-term borrowing or lending (less than a year) with lower interest rates compared to other markets. Popular instruments in this category include Treasury Bills (T-bills), Certificates of Deposit (CDs), Commercial Paper, municipal notes, Repurchase Agreements and Money Market Funds.

Trading strategies

A trading strategy is a set of rules that a trader applies in making trade decisions. This will include confluences in entering a trade, exit levels and risk management.

Scalping

Involves taking trades that last for very short periods. A scalper can make many trades throughout the day.

Swing trading

In this case a trader holds trades for several days or weeks.

The basics to trading the financial markets

Technical analysis

This is the evaluation and forecast of price movements for a given asset using chart patterns and trends rather than fundamental factors.

Psychology

Psychological factors that contribute to a trader's sucess include:

  1. Making poor decisions due to fear of missing out (FOMO) that leads one into rushing to trade.
  2. Being able to maintain emotional control and sticking to your plan even when market is going against your expectations.
  3. Patience to wait for the right opportunity.
  4. Holding on to loses with the hope that the market will change direction and go in one's favour.
  5. A series of wins can make one over confident leading to over risking.

Risk management

Risk management is the identifion and control of potential losses while also evaluating profit potential to ensure success in the financial markets. Risk management is vital to capital preservation.

Trading edge

Having an edge in the market means having a unique strategy that allows one to make consistent profits. Trading edge is a combination of superior analytical skills, experince, proper risk management and access to information.

Economic calendar

Traders and investors use the economic calendar to keep track of the release of important economic indicators such as interest rates decision, inflation data, employment data, GDP releases, manufacturing and services data. The release of such news is used to anticipate market volatility which can cause drastic price movements in the markets.
